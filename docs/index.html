Protest Safety Checklist

A short checklist to help people prepare before attending a protest.

Download Protest Safety Checklist (PDF)
  • Bring water, snacks, and any needed medications.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and shoes.
  • Protect your identity if needed (mask, hat).
  • Write an emergency contact number on your arm.
  • Charge your phone before leaving.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and stick with others when possible.

Postering Guide

Download a simple guide for making wheat paste and posting paper signs outdoors.

Download Wheatpaste Instructions (PDF)

Basic overview:

  • Mix 1 cup flour with 1 cup cold water until smooth.
  • Boil 3 cups water in a pot.
  • Slowly stir the flour mixture into the boiling water.
  • Simmer while stirring until the paste thickens.
  • Let it cool before using.
  • Brush paste on the surface, apply the poster, then brush a thin layer on top.

Support Organizations

If you want to support migrant justice and community defense work, consider donating to organizations doing local and national support.

  • Farmworker Association of Florida – A grassroots farmworker-led organization based in Apopka that advocates for the health, safety, and rights of Florida’s agricultural workers and immigrant communities.
  • Orlando Center for Justice – A Central Florida nonprofit providing legal services and advocacy for immigrants and other vulnerable communities.
  • National Bail Fund Network – Immigration Bond Fund – Supports immigration bond funds across the country that help people detained by immigration authorities secure release from detention.
  • Healing Our Homeland Bail Fund – A community bail fund that helps support individuals who may face arrest while participating in protests or community defense actions.
  • Central Florida Mutual Aid – Community mutual aid network supporting local needs including the Solidarity Fund and Trans Safety Fund for direct community support.
